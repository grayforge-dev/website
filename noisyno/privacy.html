Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 29, 2026 · Effective: May 29, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the NoisyNo mobile application ("NoisyNo", "the App", "we", "us") handles information when you use it. We designed NoisyNo to do as much as possible on your device, so the App collects very little from you.

TL;DR

  • Your audio and video files never leave your device. Noise removal runs locally.
  • We do not collect, upload, sell, or share your media files.
  • No account or sign-in required.
  • We do not request runtime permissions for microphone, camera, location, contacts, photos, or storage.
  • We collect anonymous diagnostic and usage data (crash reports, basic analytics) to keep the App stable.
  • Our sole third-party data processor is Google Firebase.

1. Information We Process #

1.1 Audio and Video Files (Processed Locally Only)

When you select a file, NoisyNo reads it from your device's storage to apply noise removal. This processing happens entirely on your device. The original file, the cleaned output, and any intermediate data are not uploaded to any server and are not shared with any third party. They remain in your device's local storage or temporary cache until you delete them or the OS clears the cache.

1.2 Files You Choose to Share

Tapping the Share button invokes the operating system's standard share sheet. Where the file goes (e.g., Messages, Mail, Google Drive) is your choice, and that service's own privacy policy applies. NoisyNo itself does not transmit the file anywhere.

1.3 Diagnostics and Analytics

To keep NoisyNo stable and understand which features are used, we send anonymous diagnostic and usage data to Google Firebase. Each service is enabled by default.

ServiceWhat it collectsPurpose
Firebase Crashlytics Crash stack traces; device model, OS version, app version; a randomly generated installation ID; basic diagnostic context (e.g., file duration, output format). Identify and fix crashes
Firebase Analytics Anonymous usage events (e.g., processing started, processing succeeded, errors); approximate country; device type; app version. Advertising ID collection is disabled. Understand feature usage and app performance
Firebase Remote Config No user data collected; the App fetches configuration values we publish. Adjust App behavior without a new build

Analytics data is anonymous — tied to a randomly generated installation identifier, not to your name, email, or media files. We never combine this data with your audio or video content.

1.4 What We Do Not Collect

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or contacts
  • Your precise location — only approximate country derived from IP, via Firebase Analytics
  • Audio or video files you did not explicitly select for noise removal
  • Advertising identifiers (AdId, IDFA, SSAID)
  • Payment information (handled entirely by Apple or Google)

2. Permissions (None Required) #

NoisyNo requests zero runtime permissions. No permission dialog ever appears — not for microphone, camera, location, contacts, photos, storage, or background activity.

You pick a file through the OS file picker (UIDocumentPicker on iOS, Storage Access Framework on Android), which grants access to just the file you tap without showing a permission prompt. The cleaned output is written into the App's own sandboxed storage, which also requires no permission.

3. Retention and Deletion #

  • Your audio/video files: kept locally on your device until you delete them or clear the App's storage. We have no copy.
  • Picker cache copies: after a successful denoise, NoisyNo deletes its temporary copy of the imported file to minimize on-device footprint. The original file is never modified.
  • Crash reports (Crashlytics): retained per Google's defaults (up to 90 days).
  • Analytics events: retained per Google's defaults (up to 14 months).

NoisyNo uses the following third-party services. Their processing is subject to their own privacy policies.

Other libraries bundled in the App (file picker, share sheet, routing, etc.) only call platform APIs locally and do not send data to us or to third-party servers.

NoisyNo does not sell personal information and does not share it for advertising purposes.

5. Your Choices and Rights #

  • Delete local data: delete the App, or clear the App's storage in device settings.
  • Limit analytics: on iOS, use Settings → Privacy & Security. On Android, use Settings → Google → Ads. (NoisyNo already opts out of AdId collection.)
  • Request deletion of diagnostic data: email grayforge.dev+noisyno@gmail.com with your device model and approximate date of use.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional legal rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to certain processing of personal data. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email above. We will respond within the period required by applicable law.

6. Children's Privacy #

NoisyNo is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

7. Changes to This Policy #

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
