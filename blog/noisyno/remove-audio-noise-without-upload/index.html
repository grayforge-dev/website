Why a no-upload approach matters

Most online noise removers upload your file to a server before processing it. But a recording can carry more than you'd expect — meeting contents, client names, an unreleased interview — so even "remove the noise" comes down to where the file is processed. A no-upload approach is worth reaching for first when:

  • Company meetings, classes, or interviews are sensitive to share
  • It's an unreleased video draft or podcast master
  • Wi-Fi is flaky — a plane, subway, or on-site spot
  • You just want it cleaned fast, with no sign-up or upload step
Typical online tool

Upload, then process

The file is sent to a website or server before it's processed. Large files take time to upload, and you have to check where and how the file is handled.

On-device approach

Processed inside your phone

Processing runs on the device. With NoisyNo, the file you pick and the result file are not uploaded to a GrayForge server.

How to remove noise from a recording in NoisyNo — 5 steps

NoisyNo works as a simple flow: pick a file → preview → clean → compare → share. You can choose a voice recording or video file and process it with no account. Here it is, step by step, with the real screens.

Step 01

Pick a recording or video file

Recent files are right there — no folder digging. In the Files tab, search by name or pick a voice memo, interview, meeting, lecture, or video; the app only touches the file you choose. Common formats work out of the box (M4A, MP3, WAV, AAC, CAF, plus MP4/MOV video), so there's nothing to convert first.

Step 02

Preview the original

Scrub the waveform and play it back to confirm it's the right clip and hear what noise is mixed in — a steady fan hum behaves differently from sudden street noise. It also sets expectations: a voice buried deep under noise gets helped but not rescued, while shaving hiss off already-clear speech lands close to a studio take.

Step 03

Run the cleanup (100% on-device)

Tap Clean and the bundled model reduces the background noise around the voice — no upload wait, all inside the device, without internet. A few minutes of audio usually finishes in seconds to a minute; keep the app open, since locking the screen or switching apps can pause processing.

Step 04

A/B the original against the result

The Original / Cleaned toggle switches between the same spot instantly, so you can hear whether the voice still sounds alive and the noise dropped naturally. It matters because aggressive removal can make a voice thin or underwater — and if the cleaned version loses too much, the original is untouched, since nothing here overwrites your source file.

Step 05

Save or share

Tap share and send the result to whichever app you want — notes, messages, a video editor, a podcast host, a transcription app. Where it goes is entirely your choice. Because there's no account, there's nothing to log into elsewhere and no library on someone else's server; the file lives where you put it.

What it fits especially well

NoisyNo focuses on making a voice easier to listen to — files where you want to keep the speech and cut the background:

  • Interviews recorded in a cafe, office, or classroom
  • Meeting recordings with laptop fan, air-con, or HVAC noise
  • Short video clips with outdoor wind in them
  • Voice memos captured in a hurry on a phone
  • First-pass voice cleanup for a podcast or short-form video

For fine-tuning music timbre, mixing multiple tracks, or professional mastering, a dedicated audio editor is a better fit.

What kinds of noise it can reduce

On-device denoising is best at steady background noise — the constant sounds that sit under a voice the whole time. Common examples people bring to NoisyNo:

  • Fan and computer hum — laptops, towers, projectors
  • Air conditioning and HVAC drone
  • Hiss and white noise — cheap mics, high gain
  • Wind — outdoor clips and breezy-day video
  • Room tone and echo — hollow, boxy rooms
  • Electrical hum and buzz — 50/60 Hz mains
  • Street and crowd noise — traffic, cafe chatter
  • Keyboard and handling noise — typing, taps, bumps

Sudden, sharp sounds — a door slam, a cough, a notification — are harder for any remover because they aren't constant. NoisyNo focuses on the steady layer that makes a voice sound "noisy," which is what most meeting, interview, and lecture recordings actually need.

Tips for the cleanest result

None of this is required — NoisyNo works on whatever you have — but a better starting point makes the voice more natural:

  • Record closer to the speaker. A voice that's louder than the room is far easier to separate from noise.
  • Avoid clipping. If the input is so loud it distorts, no tool can fully undo it — leave a little headroom.
  • One dominant noise is easier than many. A single steady hum cleans up better than a mix of wind, chatter, and echo at once.
  • Don't over-clean. If the cleaned voice starts to sound thin or metallic, the original is still there — sometimes a lighter touch sounds more human.
  • Keep the original. Save the cleaned copy alongside the source so you can always go back.

Worth knowing: "no file upload" and "no network" are different

Your recording and the result file are not uploaded to a GrayForge server — per NoisyNo's privacy policy, files are processed on the device. That's not the same as never touching the network: limited diagnostics (Firebase Crashlytics, Analytics, Remote Config) may run for app quality, and these don't include file names or audio content.

In short

NoisyNo doesn't upload your recording to process it, but the diagnostic data for stability isn't literally zero. Treat file processing and diagnostic data as two separate scopes.

Frequently asked questions

Can I remove noise from a recording without uploading it to a server?

Yes. NoisyNo is built to process the audio of a chosen voice or video file inside your phone. The file is not uploaded to a GrayForge server.

Does NoisyNo work without internet?

After installation, the core noise-removal processing runs offline. Installing the app, updates, store features, and some diagnostics may still depend on your network.

Do I need an account?

No. NoisyNo does not require an account or sign-in to remove noise from a recording.

Can it clean up the sound in a video file too?

For supported video files, it can extract the audio, remove noise, and let you save or share the resulting audio.

What audio and video formats does it support?

Common audio formats such as M4A, MP3, WAV, AAC, and CAF work directly, and NoisyNo can take the audio from MP4 and MOV video. You don't need to convert the file first.

Is NoisyNo free?

Yes. NoisyNo is free to use with no accounts, no ads, and no file uploads. You can check current details on the App Store or Google Play listing.
